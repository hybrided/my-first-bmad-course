Session 2: The Main Time Management Techniques
Duration: 20 minutes
Objective: Introduce three practical time management techniques that students can start using immediately to improve productivity and reduce procrastination.

Introduction (2 minutes)
Welcome back! In this session, we'll dive into three actionable techniques: Pomodoro, Time Blocking, and the 2-Minute Rule. These are simple tools to help you take control of your time. We'll break each one down with what it is, how to do it, when to use it, and a real student example. Let's get started!

Technique 1: Pomodoro (5 minutes)
What it is
Pomodoro is a time management method that breaks work into short, focused intervals called "pomodoros," typically 25 minutes, followed by a short break. It helps maintain concentration and prevents burnout by encouraging regular rest.

How to do it (step by step)
Choose a task to work on.
Set a timer for 25 minutes and work uninterrupted.
When the timer rings, take a 5-minute break—stretch, walk, or grab a snack.
After four pomodoros, take a longer break of 15-30 minutes.
Repeat as needed.
When to use it
Use Pomodoro for tasks that require deep focus, like studying for exams, writing papers, or tackling complex assignments. It's great when you feel distracted or overwhelmed by a big project.

Student example
Jake, a freshman, used Pomodoro to study for his biology midterm. He set his timer and focused solely on one chapter for 25 minutes, then took a break to check his phone. By the end of the day, he covered three chapters without feeling exhausted, and his grades improved.

Technique 2: Time Blocking (5 minutes)
What it is
Time Blocking is a scheduling technique where you divide your day into dedicated blocks of time for specific activities, treating them like appointments in your calendar. It helps create structure and ensures important tasks get done.

How to do it (step by step)
Review your daily or weekly tasks and priorities.
Open your calendar or planner and assign specific time slots to each task (e.g., 9-10 AM for studying).
Include buffers for breaks, meals, and unexpected events.
Stick to the blocks as much as possible, but be flexible if needed.
At the end of the day, review what worked and adjust for tomorrow.
When to use it
Use Time Blocking for planning your week, especially when you have multiple deadlines or commitments. It's ideal for students balancing classes, work, and social life to avoid last-minute cramming.

Student example
Sofia, a sophomore with a busy schedule, started time blocking her week. She blocked 7-8 PM for homework, 8-9 PM for dinner with friends, and weekends for relaxation. This helped her submit assignments on time and reduced her stress levels significantly.

Technique 3: 2-Minute Rule (5 minutes)
What it is
The 2-Minute Rule states that if a task takes less than two minutes to complete, do it immediately instead of putting it off. It's a quick way to clear small tasks and build momentum against procrastination.

How to do it (step by step)
When a small task pops up (like responding to an email or tidying your desk), ask: "Will this take less than 2 minutes?"
If yes, do it right away without delay.
If no, schedule it for later or break it into smaller steps.
Repeat consistently to prevent small tasks from piling up.
When to use it
Use the 2-Minute Rule for everyday chores, quick communications, or minor decisions that you might otherwise procrastinate on. It's perfect for maintaining a clean workspace or staying on top of small responsibilities.

Student example
Liam, who often delayed replying to group chat messages, adopted the 2-Minute Rule. Instead of letting notifications build up, he responded immediately to short messages. This freed up his mental space and helped him focus better on his major assignments.
