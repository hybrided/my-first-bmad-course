# Session 1: What is Aerospace Engineering

## Duration
20 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the basic idea of aerospace engineering
- Recognize the difference between aerospace systems and everyday objects
- Identify the main goals of aerospace engineering

## Key Concepts
- Aerospace engineering is the design, testing, and production of vehicles that fly or travel in space.
- It covers both atmosphere-based flight and space travel.
- Engineers in this field solve problems about speed, stability, strength, and safety.

## Topics
### What aerospace engineering studies
- Aircraft: planes, helicopters, drones
- Space vehicles: rockets, satellites, space stations
- Systems: propulsion, structures, controls

### Why aerospace engineering matters
- Enables travel and communication around the world
- Supports weather observation, GPS, and scientific missions
- Helps explore space and improve life on Earth

## Practical Example
- Example: An aerospace engineer may design the wing of a small drone so it stays stable in wind, carries a camera, and uses less battery power.

## Exercise
- Think of one flying device you use or know about. Write down what it needs to do well (for example, carry people, stay stable, move fast, or save fuel).

## Discussion
- Ask the group: What is one cool thing you would want an aerospace engineer to build?
- Share ideas and compare which features are the most important for different vehicles.

## Quiz
1. What is the main focus of aerospace engineering?
2. Name one example of an aerospace system in the sky.
3. Why do aerospace engineers care about safety?

## Summary
- Aerospace engineering is the study of flying and space vehicles.
- It includes aircraft, rockets, and the systems that make them work.
- The field is about solving real problems to make travel and exploration safer and more reliable.
