# Session 2: Difference Between Aerospace and Aeronautical Engineering

## Duration
20 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Learn how aerospace and aeronautical engineering are related
- Understand what makes each field different
- Identify examples of work in both areas

## Key Concepts
- Aeronautical engineering focuses on vehicles that fly within Earth’s atmosphere.
- Aerospace engineering includes both aeronautical and space engineering.
- The two fields share many basics, but aerospace adds space travel and satellites.

## Topics
### Aeronautical engineering
- Works with airplanes, helicopters, gliders, and drones
- Studies air flow, wings, engines, and landing systems
- Designs for takeoff, flight, and landing inside the atmosphere

### Aerospace engineering
- Includes aeronautical topics plus rockets, spacecraft, and satellites
- Studies conditions outside the atmosphere, like vacuum and orbit
- Designs systems that work in space and during launch

## Practical Example
- Example: A student pilot’s airplane falls under aeronautical engineering, while a communication satellite is an aerospace engineering project.

## Exercise
- List one example each of an aeronautical project and an aerospace project.
- Explain one difference that makes the aerospace example special.

## Discussion
- Group question: Which field would you choose if you want to design a new passenger jet? Which if you want to send a robot to Mars?
- Compare answers and note how the same engineer skills apply in both cases.

## Quiz
1. What does aeronautical engineering focus on?
2. Name one type of vehicle studied in aerospace engineering but not in aeronautical engineering.
3. Why is space engineering different from airplane engineering?

## Summary
- Aeronautical engineering is about flight inside Earth’s atmosphere.
- Aerospace engineering covers both air travel and space travel.
- The two fields overlap, but aerospace is broader because it includes spacecraft and satellites.
