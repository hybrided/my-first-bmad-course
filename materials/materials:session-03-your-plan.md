Session 3: Creating Your Personal Time Plan
Duration: 20 minutes
Objective: Guide students through a hands-on exercise to build a personalized weekly time management plan, helping them apply the techniques from previous sessions.

Introduction (2 minutes)
Hello again! In this final session, we're going to get practical. You'll create your own weekly schedule template—a simple tool to organize your time. We'll walk through it step by step as a group exercise. Grab a notebook, your phone's calendar app, or a piece of paper. Let's turn what we've learned into action!

Hands-On Exercise: Building Your Weekly Schedule Template (15 minutes)
Follow these steps to create a basic template. We'll do this together in class—I'll guide you, and you can ask questions as we go.

Step 1: List Your Fixed Commitments (3 minutes)
Write down all non-negotiable items in your week, like class times, work shifts, meals, and sleep. Be honest—include everything that takes up time regularly.
Example: Monday 9-10 AM: Math class; Daily 7-8 PM: Dinner.

Step 2: Identify Priorities and Goals (3 minutes)
Think about your top priorities: academics, health, social life, hobbies. Set 2-3 SMART goals for the week (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
Example: Complete reading for history class by Wednesday; Exercise 3 times this week.

Step 3: Block Out Time for Key Activities (5 minutes)
Using Time Blocking, assign time slots to your priorities and goals. Include buffers for breaks and flexibility. Incorporate techniques like Pomodoro for study blocks.
Example: Tuesday 2-4 PM: Study block (use Pomodoro); Thursday 6-7 PM: Gym time.

Step 4: Add Quick Tasks with the 2-Minute Rule (2 minutes)
Scan your list for small tasks (under 2 minutes) and assign them to existing blocks or quick slots.
Example: During lunch break: Reply to emails.

Step 5: Review and Finalize Your Template (2 minutes)
Look over your schedule. Does it feel realistic? Adjust as needed. Here's a simple template you can copy:

Time	Monday	Tuesday	Wednesday	Thursday	Friday	Saturday	Sunday
8-9 AM	Breakfast	Breakfast	Breakfast	Breakfast	Breakfast	Sleep in	Sleep in
9-10 AM	Class	Class	Class	Class	Class	Free time	Free time
10-11 AM	Study	Study	Study	Study	Study	Hobby	Hobby
11 AM-12 PM	Break	Break	Break	Break	Break	Lunch	Lunch
...	...	...	...	...	...	...	...
Fill in your slots based on Steps 1-4.

Summary (2 minutes)
Great job! You've just created a personalized time plan. This template is your starting point—tweak it as you go. Remember, it's not set in stone; flexibility is key. Use this to build better habits and reduce stress.

Action Items for After Class (1 minute)
Implement your schedule: Start using your weekly template tomorrow. Track how it feels after the first day.
Review weekly: At the end of the week, note what worked and what didn't. Adjust for the next week.
Add one technique: Pick one from Session 2 (Pomodoro, Time Blocking, or 2-Minute Rule) and incorporate it daily. Reflect on the impact in a quick journal entry.
